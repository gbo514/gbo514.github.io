Clubs, DeCals, and Resources

My thoughts to Berkeley clubs and resources for GBO group 514.

DISCOVER

Where to find clubs?

Below are three places where you can find clubs. All clubs are different - some you have to apply, where others you don't.

Shortlinks

Click this link to get to Berkeley's list of organizations. The organization categories are abstracted away and it includes not just clubs but a lot of other things too: frats, sports, etc.

CalLink

This is a list of EVERY active club on campus. I recommend looking through here if you are serious if you want to find the club for you. Be warned because there are 1000+ clubs on campus.

ASUC

The ASUC, also known as the Associated Students of the University of California, is the student government of UC Berkeley. It's one of the biggest student government systems in the world.

DeCals

Democratic Education at California, or DeCal for short, is a series of student taught classes for 1-3 units. They are very diverse in terms of topics, ranging from Pokemon to academic subjects.

Greek Life

There has been some controversy around frats and sororities lately, but if you are still interested, here is a link to them that includes all the campus partnered Greek life organizations.

Find grade distributions to classes

You probably already know about RateMyProfessors already, but did you know that there is a site where you can find ACCURATE information to Berkeley classes' grade distributions? Checkout BerkeleyTime.

Check it Out

Where do you find tutoring?

You might be wondering where you get tutoring. There is a lot of tutoring resources at Berkeley, and the biggest tutoring center on campus is the SLC, student learning center. You can schedule free tutoring sessions at the SLC, and I highly recommend it.
